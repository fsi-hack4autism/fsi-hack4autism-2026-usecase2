# Life Skills Advocate - Comprehensive IEP Goal Bank_1

## Category Summary

This comprehensive IEP goal bank organizes 1,300+ goals across 78 skill areas in the following main categories:

1. **Adaptive/Independent Living** — Functional life skills essential for independence and community participation. Includes daily living skills (meal preparation, kitchen use, time management), community skills (using public transportation, making purchases, engaging in community activities), laundry management (sorting, washing, folding), hygiene and personal care (grooming, bathing, dressing, choosing weather-appropriate clothing), household management (cleaning, organizing, storage systems), cooking and food preparation (following recipes, using appliances safely), gross motor skills (balance, coordination, shoe-tying, mobility), safety awareness (recognizing hazards, responding to emergencies, food safety, social distancing), and world knowledge (weather, seasons, current events).

2. **Communication** — All aspects of how the student expresses themselves and understands others. Receptive language covers understanding spoken instructions (single-step and multi-step), following verbal sequences, and processing speed accommodations. Pragmatics addresses social communication conventions including conversation skills, turn-taking, recognizing social cues, appropriate greetings, non-verbal communication (facial expressions, eye contact, gestures), understanding text messages and social nuance, and asking clarification questions. Expressive language includes using spoken words, picture cards, AAC devices, and complete sentences to communicate wants, needs, and ideas. Also addresses communication support strategies like visual schedules and written instructions.

3. **ELA (English Language Arts)** — Comprehensive reading and writing skills aligned with grade-level standards. Reading covers foundational skills (phoneme awareness, phonics, decoding, high-frequency word recognition, multisyllabic word pronunciation), reading for literature (comprehension, inferences, understanding figurative language, analyzing evidence), reading for informational text (summarizing, identifying key details, comparing texts, analyzing arguments, citing evidence), reading for science & technical subjects (following multi-step directions), and vocabulary development. Writing includes composing coherent sentences, organizing thoughts into paragraphs, writing informative/explanatory texts, argumentative essays with thesis statements, and research projects. Speaking & listening covers contributing to group discussions, asking follow-up questions, summarizing others' points, using purposeful gestures, listening comprehension, and determining main ideas from spoken passages.

4. **Math** — Foundational and advanced mathematical thinking. Operations & algebraic thinking covers addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, solving equations, and identifying patterns. Counting & cardinality focuses on number recognition, sequencing, and understanding quantity. Geometry includes identifying and classifying shapes (2D and 3D), understanding angles, calculating area and perimeter, using symmetry, and constructing geometric figures. Measurement & data covers estimating and measuring length, using measuring tools (protractors, rulers), comparing measurements, and collecting/organizing data. Expressions & equations involves simplifying expressions with exponents and solving algebraic equations. Functions focuses on building linear models and interpreting slope and intercept. The Number System covers place value, fractions, decimals, and division algorithms.

5. **Social/Behavior** — Social competence and behavioral expectations. Safety includes following social-distancing protocols, understanding personal space, and recognizing unsafe situations. Social skills cover interaction norms, peer relationships, cooperation, and participation in group settings. Behavior management addresses emotional regulation, coping with frustration, following rules and routines, responding to corrections, and managing transitions.

Each goal includes measurable criteria (specific accuracy percentages or frequency targets), achievement timelines (by end of IEP period), and assessment methods (task analysis, teacher observation, rubrics, checklists, data collection). Goals are mapped to Common Core State Standards (CCSS) where applicable, with codes and grade band descriptors for alignment with grade-level expectations.
